Operational components of the accountability chain. Not a civil identity. Not a replacement of national documents. Only enforceable responsibility.

Minimal fail-closed chain

The BioCyber Identity Card is implemented as a minimal chain. If any component is missing, the system must not proceed.

Event → Human Root Reference (HRR) → Decision Gate Validator (DGV) → Certified Time & Trace → Long-Term Evidence Vault
Fail-closed rule: if attribution, validation, time/trace or long-term evidence cannot be guaranteed, the outcome must be FAIL_CLOSED.

Core components

Component 01

Human Root Reference (HRR)

Technical human attribution. Guarantees that a real, accountable human exists behind a critical action.

  • unique per individual
  • not duplicable
  • auditable
  • suspendable (not erasable)
Attribution Audit Non-duplication
Component 02

Decision Gate Validator (DGV)

Validates or blocks actions before execution, enforcing a standard decision outcome.

  • mandatory fail-closed behavior
  • standard outcomes: ALLOW / REVIEW / DENY / FAIL_CLOSED
  • machine-readable logs
Pre-execution gate Fail-closed Logging
Component 03

Certified Time & Trace

Metrologically valid time and traceability. What cannot be measured and traced is not enforceable.

  • certified timestamps with known error bounds
  • traceability and reproducible verification
  • consistent reference time source
Time authority Traceability Verification
Component 04

Long-Term Evidence Vault

Long-term preservation of evidence, designed to remain usable and verifiable over decades.

  • evidence retention ≥ 20 years
  • offline verification
  • periodic re-timestamping
  • ETSI / eIDAS compatible LTA profiles
LTA Offline verify Evidence

Phase 1 scope

This phase targets high-risk domains where accountability failures have high cost:

  • AI systems under EU AI Act (high-risk use cases)
  • critical healthcare systems
  • C2 / C4ISR environments
  • automated public procurement

Low-risk consumer use cases are out of scope.
