EU Technical Position

European Responsibility Infrastructure

Technical positioning document for a verifiable responsibility layer for high-impact digital and physical systems within the European context.

Structural problem

Digital and automated systems increasingly perform actions with real-world consequences. Artificial intelligence, robotics, distributed computation and automated infrastructures operate across jurisdictions and organizational boundaries.

When a critical action occurs, attribution of responsibility often remains ambiguous. Existing identity systems identify actors but do not provide a deterministic and publicly verifiable link between an action and a responsible entity at the moment of execution.

This ambiguity generates legal friction, operational risk and increased audit cost.

Technical gap

Current European frameworks address privacy, data protection and cybersecurity. However, a standardized mechanism for ex-ante responsibility verification before execution of high-impact actions remains absent.

A technical layer capable of binding identity, responsibility and time in a verifiable and privacy-preserving manner would reduce disputes, increase traceability and improve auditability across sectors.

IPR approach

The Identity Primary Record model introduces a minimal, hash-based reference linking an entity to a specific responsibility context.

Verification occurs without centralized identity storage. Only cryptographic references are required for public verification.

If verification cannot be completed, execution may be considered invalid under a fail-closed logic.

Potential application domains

Artificial intelligence decision systems
Robotics and autonomous physical agents
Distributed datacenter execution
Critical infrastructure operations
Cross-border digital governance

Positioning

This infrastructure does not replace legal identity frameworks and does not introduce centralized identity databases.

It operates as an additional technical layer for verifiable attribution and responsibility binding, compatible with privacy-preserving principles.

The model is compatible with an incremental pilot approach and does not require immediate regulatory integration.
